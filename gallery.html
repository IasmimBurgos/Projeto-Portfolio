"Children of the Gods"

"Children of the Gods" explores the aura of sensuality emanating from the depicted figures, with the highlight of the artwork being their intense, penetrating gazes. These looks invite the viewer to immerse themselves in the scene, creating a profound and personal connection with the context of the piece.

"Sacred Cradle"

"Sacred Cradle" symbolizes life and death, the beginning and the end, intertwined as inevitable parts of a predetermined fate. Its melancholic aura reflects both the denial and acceptance of a path imposed upon you. It represents the duality that permeates all things, where time and existence intersect, revealing the fragility and inevitability of life's cycle.

21 de set

"Camp"

"Camp" is a work that depicts the last day of camping for a group of friends, before their souls find rest. The piece captures the mix of nostalgia and sadness that permeates this moment, with smiles and laughter masking the inevitability of separation. The details of the natural environment, illuminated by the soft glow of the campfire, create an atmosphere of reflection and serenity. Through the expressions and interactions of the characters, I invite the viewer to feel the depth of shared memories and the significance of the bonds formed, even in the face of farewell.

"Soulmates"

"Soulmates" captures youth in its purest state: the touch, the gaze, and the sweetness of love. It is an intimate portrait of two young individuals, two completely passionate souls intertwined in a dance of genuine and intense emotions. The piece evokes the essence of emotional connection, celebrating the beauty and vulnerability of young love.

21 de set

"Ars Goetia"

"Ars Goetia" is a work that explores the mystical and the occult, inspired by the famous grimoire that lists the 72 demons. The piece creates an enigmatic atmosphere that invites the viewer to immerse themselves in a universe where the supernatural meets reality. The vibrant colors and intricate details reflect the complexity of emotions and the forces that govern human nature. Through this work, I aim to provoke reflections on power, control, and the pursuit of knowledge, challenging the boundaries between the sacred and the profane.
